Research Projects

Ice sheets and glaciers in a warming climate: measurement and modelling of surface melt lakes

2026–2029. This project aims to increase our understanding of how surface meltwater lakes form, evolve, and drain in a warming climate. We will study how the combination of varying atmospheric forcings and snowpack properties influence surface lake development, as well as investigating the links between lake drainage and subglacial water storage potential. Site-specific field measurements will be used for model validation to allow for extrapolation across various regions.

This project is funded within the CROCUS Doctoral Landscape Award Scheme and also receives support from the University of Maine Polar Geophysics research team.



Tere Tīpako Tio: Rapid Extensive Ice Sampling Aotearoa

2022–2025. The goal of this project was to develop new low-cost, lightweight, easy-to-deploy ice sampling equipment. The advent of these instruments will, over time, make a step change in the ability of the scientific and geotechnical community to collect ice physical property data to improve the quality of ice sheet modelling, reducing uncertainty in projections.

This project was funded by New Zealand’s Ministry of Business, Innovation and Employment (MBIE) as part of the 2022 Smart Ideas scheme. The principal investigator for Tere Tīpako Tio was David Prior, based in the Geology Department at the University of Otago.
