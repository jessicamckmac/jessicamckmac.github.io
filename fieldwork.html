Fieldwork

Juneau Icefield, Alaska

I will be heading to Juneau Icefield in Alaska to join the Juneau Icefield Research Program (JIRP) as a visiting researcher. During my time on the Icefield I will be investigating supraglacial lakes that form at the lateral margins of the Taku Glacier using multiple geophysical methods. I will also be providing hot water drilling support to other research projects across the Icefield. This research will be conducted on the lands of the Tlingit peoples, past and present.

McMurdo Ice Shelf, Antarctica

As a part of the Tere Tīpako Tio Project I spent two Antarctic seasons on the McMurdo Ice Shelf near New Zealand’s Antarctic station, Scott Base. We conducted various geophysical measurements including ground penetrating radar, seismics, aPRES, transient electromagnetics, and borehole optical imaging along with GNSS monitoring and ice sampling. Within this fieldwork I led the hot water drilling efforts as well as the development and testing of novel ice sampling equipment.

Norunda, Sweden

I have recently conducted fieldwork at the Norunda research field station in Sweden, operated by ICOS. We conducted Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) and self-potential geophysical surveying. The data collected during this fieldwork will be used to help understand the effect clear cutting boreal forests has on groundwater flow and greenhouse gas emissions.

New Zealand

During my time working at Southern Geophysical Ltd I conducted numerous field campaigns all across the country. Some highlights include ERT surveying on remote farmland in the North Island, ground penetrating radar on Stewart Island, and coastal geophysics in Southland.
